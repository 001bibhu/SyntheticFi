# Overview

SyntheticFi is a financial infrastructure platform that helps advisors and investors access **liquidity**—cash—using an investment portfolio as **collateral**, without selling the underlying assets.

This page explains what SyntheticFi is, who uses it, and how it fits into a broader financial plan. No prior knowledge of advanced trading strategies is required.

---

## What is SyntheticFi?

SyntheticFi is **not a traditional lender**.

Traditional lenders evaluate your credit score, income, and debt ratios. They may require a home or other assets as collateral. SyntheticFi works differently: it uses your **investment portfolio**—stocks, ETFs, and similar holdings—as the basis for structured financing.

In plain terms:

1. You (or your client) need cash.
2. You do not want to sell investments, which can trigger taxes and disrupt a long-term strategy.
3. SyntheticFi structures financing backed by the portfolio, delivers cash upfront, and sets a fixed cost for repayment later.

SyntheticFi handles the **structuring and infrastructure**—connecting to brokerages and custodians, managing collateral, and coordinating the financing lifecycle. Your advisor or firm remains the primary relationship for the client.

| User type | How they use SyntheticFi |
|-----------|--------------------------|
| **Financial advisors** (RIAs, wealth managers, broker-dealers) | Offer portfolio-based liquidity as part of holistic planning; onboard clients; monitor positions and margin status |
| **High-net-worth investors** | Access cash for major expenses, taxes, or opportunities without liquidating holdings |
| **Mortgage brokers & financial service providers** | Integrate portfolio liquidity into home purchases, bridge financing, or cross-sell workflows |

SyntheticFi is built for sophisticated investors and the professionals who serve them. Minimum portfolio requirements and eligibility rules apply.

## Why SyntheticFi exists

Investors often face a common dilemma: **they have wealth on paper, but need cash now**.

Selling investments solves the cash problem but creates new ones:

- **Capital gains taxes** on appreciated assets
- **Lost future growth** from exiting positions
- **Timing risk** if markets recover after a sale
- **Strategy disruption** for concentrated or legacy holdings

SyntheticFi exists to offer a middle path: **liquidity without liquidation**. By using the portfolio as collateral and structuring financing through public markets, investors can meet near-term cash needs while keeping their investment strategy intact.

## High-level workflow

Here is what happens from start to finish, at a high level:

```
1. Connect portfolio     →  Link brokerage or custodian account
2. Review eligibility    →  Confirm holdings, value, and financing terms
3. Structure financing   →  SyntheticFi sets up the collateral arrangement
4. Receive cash          →  Funds are delivered to the designated account
5. Manage during term    →  Monitor portfolio value and margin status
6. Repay and close       →  Repay at the agreed cost; collateral is released
```

Each step involves verification, disclosures, and compliance checks. Timelines vary by account type and integration.

## Key concepts

These terms appear throughout the documentation. Each is explained simply here; deeper detail is in [How it works](./how-it-works.md).

### Liquidity

**Liquidity** means access to cash you can spend or transfer. An investment portfolio has economic value, but it is not liquid until you sell assets or borrow against them. SyntheticFi converts portfolio value into usable cash.

### Collateral

**Collateral** is an asset pledged to secure financing. If you cannot meet repayment or margin requirements, the collateral may be used to satisfy obligations. With SyntheticFi, your **portfolio holdings** serve as collateral—not your home or personal property (unless separately agreed in a specific program).

### Locking

When financing is active, a portion of the portfolio may be **locked**—meaning those holdings cannot be sold or transferred freely until the financing is repaid or adjusted. Locking protects both the investor and the financing structure. See [How it works](./how-it-works.md#what-locking-means) for details.

### Fixed cost

SyntheticFi structures financing with a **known cost** for the term—not open-ended interest that compounds unpredictably. Exact terms depend on portfolio size, duration, and market conditions at origination.

### Margin risk

If the portfolio **drops in value**, the collateral may no longer fully cover the financing. This creates **margin risk**: you may need to add collateral, repay partially, or adjust the structure. SyntheticFi monitors this continuously and notifies you before forced actions when possible.

## How SyntheticFi differs from traditional loans

| Traditional loan | SyntheticFi |
|------------------|-------------|
| Based on credit score and income | Based on portfolio value and eligibility |
| Often secured by real estate or personal assets | Secured by investment portfolio |
| Generic bank product | Structured financing via public markets |
| May require lengthy underwriting | Streamlined for qualified portfolios |
| Selling investments not involved | Designed to avoid selling investments |

SyntheticFi is **financial infrastructure**, not a replacement for every type of borrowing. Home mortgages, small-business SBA loans, and unsecured personal credit serve different purposes. SyntheticFi is optimized for **investors who want liquidity without disrupting their portfolio**.

## What happens behind the scenes (without the jargon)

SyntheticFi structures financing using established public-market mechanisms—including options-based strategies such as box spreads—to create a predictable cash outcome tied to portfolio collateral. You do not need to understand these instruments to use the platform; your advisor or SyntheticFi's team handles execution.

If you want a deeper explanation of the lifecycle, collateral mechanics, and margin calls, continue to [How it works](./how-it-works.md).
